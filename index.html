About Me

I am a mechanical design engineer with significant experience across robotics, consumer electronics, and medical product development. Strengths include advanced SolidWorks, design of electromechanical systems, and a drive to learn new skills, especially in electronics and controls.

High-Level Skills

Conceptual & Detail Design

I have engaged in many design projects at the very early, idea-on-a-napkin stage and carried them through to production drawing generation. Most projects were done in SolidWorks, though I also have experience with CREO Parametric and OnShape. I was reponsible for concept ideation, material selection, COTS part sourcing, vendor contacts, etc.

Prototyping

I have developed prototypes of varying complexity: from specific conceptual, one-off demos to explore a given mechanism, material, or process, to highly-detailed, functional full systems. I have experience operating 3D printers, laser cutters, an OMAX water jet cutter, and have led engagement with many manufacturing vendors, both domestic and abroad for part procurement.

Design for Manufacturing

Starting from my 8+ years in the FIRST Robotics community, I have accrued substantial first-hand machining and assembly experience. That, coupled with the invaluable discussions and feedback I have received from professional machinists and more senior engineers, has all fed into my design for manufacturing approach. This has given me insight into effective ways to optimize features for machinability, keep part cost down, and streamline production timelines.

Business Development

I am the main point of contact with potential clients and new leads at NK Labs. I evaluate how each project fits with NK Labs capabilities, timelines, and feasibility. Then, I coordinate detail meetings with relevant staff, and draw up proposals, quotes, and grant applications.

Bicycle Light Mount

2021

I bought a new headlight for my bike, and it was great. Then I bought a new bike, and it was also great. Putting the two together - not great. The mount that comes with the light cannot fit on 31.8mm handlebars. I was devastated. So I spent way too much time designing this modular mount. My light is held securely, but it is still easily removable. The top parts are also removable, so attachments for other lights or other items can be designed to work with the bar-clamping parts in the future. Here's to many well-lit rides!

Two-Source Diffusion Simulation

Summer 2014

My partner and I chose to pursue this task as our final project for our International Biomedical Optics course in Taipei, Taiwan. The purpose of this graduate-level research was to find a relationship between the concentration of blood in bone and its effect on detected power. We used MATLAB and MCML modeling to simulate various conditions including ranges of wavelengths, blood concentrations, and distances to detectors. Ultimately, we found that increasing blood concentration has a large effect on the increase in detected power.
