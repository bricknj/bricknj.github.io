Project Repo

Bicycle Light Mount

2021 | Personal Project

(Onshape, FDM Printing)

I bought a new bike light, and it was great. Then, I bought a new bike, and it was also great. Putting the two together - not great. The mount that comes with the light could not fit on 31.8mm handlebars. I was devastated.

So I designed this modular handlebar mount in Onshape. I printed the parts on a Markforged FDM printer for high- strength, as these parts would see a lot of wear and tear on my bike. My light is now held securely, but it is still easily removable. The upper parts are also replaceable, so attachments for other lights or other items can be designed to work with the bar-clamping parts in the future. Here's to many well-lit rides!

Final Presentation

Two-Source Diffusion Simulation

Summer 2014 | Northeastern

(MATLAB)

My partner and I chose to pursue this task as our final project for our Biomedical Optics course in Taipei, Taiwan. The purpose of this graduate-level research was to find a relationship between the concentration of blood in bone and its effect on detected power as light penetrated through our two-layer model of skin and bone. We used MATLAB and MCML modeling to simulate various conditions, including ranges of wavelengths, blood concentrations, and distances to detectors. Ultimately, we found that increasing blood concentration has a large effect on the increase in detected power.
